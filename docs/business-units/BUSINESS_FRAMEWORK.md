# Business Units Framework
## Global Parikrama Harīm - Cottage Industries and Business Structure

## Overview

The Global Parikrama Harīm operates as a network of dynamic, mobile cottage industries. Each member maintains their own business while contributing to collective enterprises. This framework ensures coordination, mutual support, and sustainable economic vitality.

## Core Principles

### 1. Individual Entrepreneurship
- **Personal Business**: Every member develops and maintains their own business
- **Skill Development**: Continuous learning and mastery
- **Creative Expression**: Business as art and performance
- **Economic Autonomy**: Personal financial sovereignty

### 2. Collective Enterprise
- **Shared Resources**: Pooled tools, materials, and infrastructure
- **Collaborative Projects**: Joint ventures and partnerships
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Open exchange of skills and techniques
- **Mutual Support**: Help during challenges or transitions

### 3. Mobile Operations
- **Location Independence**: Businesses designed for travel
- **Portable Equipment**: Minimal, versatile tools and materials
- **Digital Integration**: Online presence and remote operations
- **Market Adaptation**: Flexibility to serve different locations

## Business Unit Structure

### Individual Business Plan
Each member develops:
1. **Business Description**: Clear articulation of services/products
2. **Skills Inventory**: Current abilities and development goals
3. **Resource Needs**: Tools, materials, space requirements
4. **Market Strategy**: Customer identification and outreach
5. **Financial Planning**: Pricing, income goals, expense management
6. **Integration Plan**: How business supports pilgrimage lifestyle

### Collective Business Coordination

#### Business Circles
- **Regular Meetings**: Weekly or bi-weekly coordination
- **Resource Allocation**: Sharing tools and materials
- **Project Collaboration**: Joint ventures and partnerships
- **Problem Solving**: Support for challenges
- **Celebration**: Acknowledging successes

#### Shared Infrastructure
- **Mobile Workshop**: Portable tools and equipment
- **Digital Platforms**: Website, social media, e-commerce
- **Storage**: Secure storage for materials and inventory
- **Transportation**: Vehicles for goods and equipment
- **Communication**: Shared contact information and scheduling

## Economic Model

### Individual Income
- **Personal Earnings**: Members keep income from individual businesses
- **Pricing Autonomy**: Each person sets own prices
- **Financial Privacy**: Personal finances remain private
- **Growth Support**: Collective helps expand individual businesses

### Collective Contributions
- **Tithe System**: Voluntary contributions (suggested 10-20%)
- **Purpose**: Support collective needs (travel, nursery, shared resources)
- **Transparency**: Clear accounting of collective funds
- **Democratic Allocation**: Collective decides on expenditures

### Currency Integration
- **Multiple Currencies**: Accept various forms of payment
- **Harīm Currency**: Internal exchange using collective currency system
- **Barter and Trade**: Exchange of goods and services
- **Gift Economy**: Generosity and reciprocity
