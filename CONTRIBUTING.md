# Contributing to Global Parikrama Harīm

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Global Parikrama Harīm governance framework!

## How to Contribute

### For Members

Members of the Harīm can contribute by:

1. **Documentation Improvements**
   - Clarifying existing frameworks
   - Adding missing details
   - Correcting errors or outdated information
   - Translating documents to other languages

2. **New Resources**
   - Templates for business plans
   - Forms and checklists
   - Educational materials
   - Operational procedures

3. **Experience Sharing**
   - Case studies from real situations
   - Lessons learned from implementations
   - Best practices discovered
   - Challenges and solutions

### For Supporters

Non-members who support our mission can contribute by:

1. **Feedback and Suggestions**
   - Reviewing frameworks for clarity
   - Identifying gaps or areas needing development
   - Offering expertise in relevant areas
   - Sharing resources from other communities

2. **Technical Support**
   - Website development
   - Documentation formatting
   - Translation services
   - Design and graphics

### Contribution Process

1. **Review Existing Materials**
   - Read relevant documentation thoroughly
   - Understand the context and principles
   - Check if similar work already exists

2. **Propose Your Contribution**
   - For major changes: Open an issue for discussion first
   - For minor fixes: Submit a pull request directly
   - Explain your reasoning and goals

3. **Align with Principles**
   - Ensure alignment with Buddha Maitreya's (Principal Visionary) vision and direction
   - Honor poly-pansexual governance principles
   - Maintain compassionate, inclusive language
   - Support mobile, sustainable operations
   - Recognize that all contributions must align with visionary authority

4. **Submit Changes**
   - Follow existing formatting and style
   - Be clear and concise
   - Provide examples when helpful
   - Reference related documents

5. **Collaborative Review**
   - Participate in discussion constructively
   - Be open to feedback and suggestions
   - Revise based on collective input
   - Seek consensus when possible

## Guidelines

### Language and Tone
- **Compassionate**: Kind, supportive language
- **Inclusive**: Welcoming all identities and backgrounds
- **Clear**: Simple, understandable writing
- **Practical**: Actionable guidance
- **Respectful**: Honoring diverse perspectives

### Content Standards
- **Accurate**: Factually correct information
- **Relevant**: Directly applicable to Harīm life
- **Comprehensive**: Thorough but not overwhelming
- **Adaptable**: Flexible for different situations
- **Ethical**: Aligned with community values

### Format and Style
- **Markdown**: Use standard markdown formatting
- **Structure**: Clear headings and organization
- **Lists**: Bullet points for readability
- **Examples**: Concrete illustrations when helpful
- **Links**: Cross-reference related documents

1. **Templates and Forms**

   - Governance meeting agendas

2. **Procedures and Protocols**

3. **Educational Resources**

   - Problem-solving stories

## Decision-Making on Contributions

### Minor Changes
- Typo fixes, formatting improvements
- Link corrections, minor clarifications
- Can be merged by any member

### Moderate Changes
- New sections, significant additions
- Policy clarifications, expanded explanations
- Require review by relevant coordinators
- May require Leadership Council review for alignment with visionary direction

### Major Changes
- New frameworks, substantial revisions
- Policy changes, structural modifications
- Require full assembly discussion and consensus
- **Require final approval by Buddha Maitreya (Principal Visionary)**
- Major documentation changes subject to visionary authority to ensure alignment with sacred mission

## Recognition

Contributors will be acknowledged in:
- Document change logs
- Annual reports
- Community gratitude ceremonies
- Contributor list (with permission)

## Questions or Support

If you have questions about contributing:
- Open an issue for public discussion
- Contact the documentation coordinator (if established)
- Bring to a community gathering
- Ask during open forums

## Code of Conduct

All contributors must:
- Act with compassion and respect
- Honor confidentiality when appropriate
- Avoid harmful or divisive content
- Support the collective mission
- Maintain integrity and honesty
- Respect the Principal Visionary's ultimate authority and direction
- Work within the established governance framework
